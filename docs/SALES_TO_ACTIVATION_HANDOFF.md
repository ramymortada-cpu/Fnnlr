# fnnlr — Sales-to-Activation Handoff

> How a qualified, agreed lead becomes a set-up customer with no confusion. The
> handoff pack is generated by `sales:handoff`; missing customer inputs appear as
> explicit placeholders, never fabricated values. No fake readiness.

## When to run the handoff
- Fit is not bad_fit, proposal accepted, agreement signed.
- Owners assigned for the critical steps (setup, support, response, payment
  confirmation, rollback) — otherwise the handoff is **BLOCKED**.

## What `sales:handoff` produces
- A setup checklist (have / missing) for the required customer inputs.
- A customer-zero config **draft** with placeholders for anything not collected.
- An execution manifest **draft** with placeholders for anything not collected.
- The list of missing customer inputs.
- Any missing owners (which block the launch).
- A launch-window suggestion (customer-confirmed).
- The next action.

## Required customer inputs (collect before setup)
WhatsApp number · payment instructions · offer promise/price · traffic source ·
launch window · public app URL · owner emails. (See the agreement's
responsibilities checklist.)

## From handoff to launch
1. Collect every placeholder (no `<<MISSING>>` left).
2. `customer:verify` then `customer:create` (idempotent).
3. Build the page + tracked link + payment method (in the app).
4. `customer:execution-lock` → launch only on READY/WARN.
5. `customer:go-live` → 72h monitor → week-1 review.

## Honest limits
fnnlr does not auto-send WhatsApp and does not process payments (manual payment
state only). Recommendations need manual approval and are evidence-based. There is
no guaranteed revenue. Customer responsibilities are explicit in the handoff and
the agreement.
