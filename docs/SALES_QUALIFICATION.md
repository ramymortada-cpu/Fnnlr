# fnnlr — Sales Qualification

> Qualify honestly. The goal is a customer fnnlr can actually help, with correct
> expectations. A customer who wrongly assumes auto-send or a sales guarantee is
> a disqualify unless they accept the real product (no auto-send, no guarantee).

## Qualify IN if the customer has
- [ ] A clear offer (promise, price, package).
- [ ] A WhatsApp sales motion (they already sell, or intend to sell, over WhatsApp).
- [ ] A traffic source (organic, ads, bio link, list — something that brings people).
- [ ] Manual / local payment (InstaPay, Vodafone Cash, bank transfer, Fawry, cash).
- [ ] A person who responds to leads.
- [ ] A person who confirms payments.
- [ ] Willingness to commit to the 7-day setup.

## Disqualify (or re-set expectations first) if the customer
- Expects fnnlr to **auto-send** WhatsApp messages — it does not.
- Expects fnnlr to **process payments** — it records manual state only.
- Expects **guaranteed sales / revenue / ROI** — there are no guarantees.
- Has no traffic source and no plan to get one — the funnel will have nothing to
  measure.
- Has no one to respond to leads — the WhatsApp motion needs a human.

## Expectation-setting (say this out loud)
"fnnlr builds your page and tracked links, records what really happens, and shows
you evidence-based next steps. It does not send messages for you and does not move
money. You approve every change. It will not promise you a number — it will show
you what's true and what to do about it."

## Decision
- **Qualified**: meets the IN list and accepts the limits.
- **Needs expectation reset**: good fit but expects auto-send / guarantees — fix
  expectations, then re-qualify.
- **Not a fit (now)**: no traffic and no responder, or unwilling to accept manual
  send / manual payment.
